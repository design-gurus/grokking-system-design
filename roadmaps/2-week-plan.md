# 2-week system design sprint

A focused plan for when you have an interview coming up soon. Assumes about 1 to 2 hours per day. Adjust to your level.

## Week 1: build the foundation

| Day | Focus |
|-----|-------|
| 1 | The [interview framework](../cheat-sheets/interview-framework.md) and [estimation](../cheat-sheets/estimation.md) |
| 2 | [Caching](../patterns/caching.md) and [load balancing](../patterns/load-balancing.md) |
| 3 | Sharding and partitioning, [replication](../patterns/replication.md) |
| 4 | Consistency models and the CAP theorem |
| 5 | Message queues and rate limiting |
| 6 | [SQL vs NoSQL](../cheat-sheets/sql-vs-nosql.md), indexing, consistent hashing |
| 7 | Review: re-explain every pattern from memory in 2 minutes each |

## Week 2: apply to questions

| Day | Focus |
|-----|-------|
| 8 | [Design TinyURL](../questions/design-tinyurl.md) end to end |
| 9 | Design Instagram or Twitter (feed and timeline) |
| 10 | Design a chat system (WhatsApp) |
| 11 | Design Uber or a geo system |
| 12 | Design a web crawler or a notification system |
| 13 | A timed mock under interview conditions |
| 14 | Review your weak spots and redo one question |

## Tips

- Always talk out loud and draw. The interview tests communication as much as knowledge.
- After each question, write down what you missed and target it next time.
- Practice live: [mock interviews with ex-FAANG engineers](https://www.designgurus.io/mock-interviews).
