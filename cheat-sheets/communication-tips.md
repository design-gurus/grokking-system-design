# Interview communication tips

The system design round tests how you think and communicate as much as what you know. Here is how to come across as a strong, senior candidate.

## Drive the conversation

- Use the [interview framework](interview-framework.md) as a visible structure, so the interviewer always knows where you are.
- State your assumptions out loud and confirm scope before designing.
- Lead, do not wait to be asked. A senior candidate steers the session.

## Think out loud

- Narrate your reasoning, not just your conclusions. The interviewer is scoring how you think.
- When you weigh two options, say both and why you pick one. This is where [trade-off](trade-offs.md) reasoning shows.

## Draw

- Sketch boxes and arrows for the high-level design. A clear diagram is worth many sentences and keeps you both oriented.

## Manage time

- Do not rabbit-hole. Cover the whole design first, then go deep where the interviewer cares.
- Keep an eye on the clock and leave time for bottlenecks and trade-offs.

## Use the interviewer

- Treat their questions and hints as signals about what they want to explore, and follow them.
- Check in: "Does it make sense to go deeper on the data model, or focus on scaling?"

## Close well

- Summarize the design, call out the main bottlenecks, and name what you would improve with more time. A strong close leaves a strong impression.
